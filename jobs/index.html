Specialist Exercise Coach to run a Trip and Fall Prevention Pilot Program

Abbeyfield Ferring has been awarded Â£10,000 by Sport England to run a program for older people in care homes and residents of Ferring to improve the health of older people through exercise sessions and develop and deliver a trip and falls prevention program.

This is a ground breaking project; the post holder will be able to:

  • Access the needs of elderly participants and develop a program to meet their needs
  • Promote and publicise the project and recruit participants
  • Deliver 150 minutes of sessions per week for the participants
  • Take baseline measurements of participants and monitor progress
  • Train staff and volunteers the skills required to deliver the program
  • Write and disseminate a post project report and evaluation.

The post holder will ideally hold a good degree, 2:1 or higher in a related discipline or be qualified to level 7, have good research and analytical skills and have experience of clinical work with older people or vulnerable groups.

The post holder will be expected to design, deliver and evaluate the project to meet the needs of the funder, work independently, manage budgets, keep accurate records, assess participants and produce a post project report and disseminate the findings.

The project will last for 12 months, working hours will be flexible but may include evenings and weekends. Good rates of pay are available to the right candidate.
